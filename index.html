Why It Pays To Do It Now

Left alone, buildup does real damage.

Roof algae (Gloeocapsa magma) feeds on the limestone in asphalt shingles and traps moisture against the roof. Over time it strips the protective granules and can cut a 25 year roof down to 15 to 20 years.1 The same organic growth holds damp against wood, which speeds up rot and locks in staining that only gets harder to remove the longer it sits.2

The longer it waits, the more it costs. Cleaning now protects the roof and wood you've already paid for, and gets this property on the calendar before the busy season fills up.
